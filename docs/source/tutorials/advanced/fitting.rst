=================
Intensity Fitting
=================

This tutorial will show you how to use the calculator to fit experimental intensity data, which lets you extract structural parameters of your sample.

Diffraction Intensity Curve
###########################
When thin films are measured in grazing incidence, the reflected intensity strongly depends on the angle of incidence (AOI).
Changing AOI result in a changing XEFI within the sample, which directly modifies the scattered X-ray intensity measured on a detector. By simulating the XEFI of a thin film structure, we can identify the **depth** at which the diffraction intensity profile belongs to, such as the bulk or surface layers.


Bulk-Surface Ratio
##################
In certain circumstances, bulk and surface diffraction can be seen simultaneously, due to differing crystal structures which are probed in grazing incidence. In this case, the ratio of the surface and bulk diffraction intensity can be compared to the XEFI at the surface and bulk layers, which can be used to extract structural parameters (bulk:surface ratio, film thickness, roughness, etc.) of the sample.
